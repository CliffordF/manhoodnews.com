Symptoms & Causes

Erectile dysfunction (ED) is a common condition that affects millions of men. Symptoms include being unable to get or keep an erection and problems with ejaculation. ED can also cause anxiety and stress, which may contribute to serious health issues like heart disease or depression. The good news? You don’t have to deal with ED on your own. For example, if you are suffering from both diabetes and hypertension, which increases your risk of cardiovascular problems; these two conditions could work together in causing erectile dysfunction. Aside from physical conditions and injuries, there are other reasons why ED may occur. One cause includes psychological issues such as depression or anxiety. Although these conditions don’t directly cause impotence on their own, they will make other causes worse; thereby increasing your chances of ED occurring. Other psychological disorders such as ADD/ADHD (attention deficit disorder/attention deficit hyperactivity disorder) can make the sexual activity less enjoyable for both partners if not properly treated before problems arise in bed during intercourse. As mentioned earlier, there are multiple different things that can lead to ED. However, luckily for those who suffer from various forms of erectile dysfunction, most types aren’t serious enough to warrant immediate medical treatment. In fact, many instances of ED clear up within a matter of weeks or months with no outside help needed at all! Take note: The best way to approach how to cure erectile dysfunction is not all at once but instead step-by-step through several different methods until one begins working again. The key here is patience because it could take several weeks before any noticeable changes take place.
